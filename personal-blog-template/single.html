What do I like about Serious games?

25-Feb-2022

Blog

2 Comments

Can games induce violence in people or are they a vice that distances us from reality to the point that we cannot discern it from fiction? Surely more than one person has heard these somewhat controversial but valid questions that have arisen in recent years with the easy access that children have to new technologies today. However, little is said about the branch of video games called "Serious Games" which are games created to fulfill a function beyond entertainment, it seeks to fulfill the mission of educating, specially in the medical sub-branch that improve the quality of life of so many patients. So, what is the impact that the use of "Serious Games" could bring to the people with medical issues or disability conditions

The Dutch company Healthy Solutions has been gaining worldwide renown over the last few years as a result of winning the Serious Game 2013 award in the health category. This award is presented at the Fun&Serious festival in Bilbao annually since 2011 as an initiative to recognize those games that seek to create entertaining solutions to some problem in the areas of Health, Education, Business Strategy and Sport.

On this occasion it was Plan-It Commander that earned them this honor, and the recognition that goes with it. This game is famous in the medical community for being a helpful tool for families with children suffering from PDAH syndrome (attention deficit hyperactivity disorder), as the physics of this video game manage to develop motivation, increase the level of graded performance (different levels), strategic behavior and stimulation of prosocial behavior without being intrusive and capturing the children's interest.

Children suffering from this syndrome have increased in recent years and are characterized by inattention or distractibility, hyperactivity and impulsive behavior that affects all their daily life environments. This is why the game seeks to improve their understanding of their environment, especially their time management and planning, through a series of challenges organized by levels that they must complete within a maximum time of minutes per day.

Consequently, the game has shown that after 10 weeks of use a considerable percentage of the children have overcome their social disadvantages in a fun way, practicing concepts such as planning, time management and prosocial behavior. In comparison to the study group who were only attending conventional therapies recurrently in the same period of time.

This work has helped many families with difficult situations marked by ADHD syndrome, facilitating the reintegration of the children suffering from ADHD into a daily environment and thus decreasing the time their families needed to invest in their special care. Many of the tutors were able to return to their jobs after the project, as they were able to reintegrate their children back into mainstream schools, improving the quality of life of the whole family unit.

Kim CM Bul, MSc; Pamela M Kato, EdM, PhD; et al. (February 16, 2016). Behavioral Outcome Effects of Serious Gaming as an Adjunct to Treatment for Children With Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder: A Randomized Controlled Trial. Retrieve from JMIR Publications: https://www.jmir.org/2016/2/e26/
